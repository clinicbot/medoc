Treatment Protocols for Androgenetic Alopecia

Dr. Ana Lyakhovitsky & Dr. Jonathan Shapiro

Male Androgenetic Alopecia (AGA)

Stepwise treatment protocol for men, beginning with topical therapy and escalating as needed.

1Topical Minoxidil 5%
  • Application: Apply twice daily to the scalp.
  • Duration: Continue for at least 6 months.
  • Evaluation: If no improvement after 6 months, proceed to systemic therapy.
2Systemic Therapy — Finasteride
  • Finasteride (Propecia) 1 mg daily.
  • Duration: Trial for one year.
  • Long-term: If effective, continue indefinitely.
3Escalation — Oral Minoxidil
  • Initial dose: 2.5 mg per day.
  • Prescription: 90 capsules.
  • Procurement options:
    • Prescription filled at regular pharmacies.
    • Order via "Tabechnik" using form "29ג" from "Shor Tabechnik".
  • Transition from topical to oral: Gradually reduce topical minoxidil from twice daily to 2–3 times weekly over 2 months while initiating oral minoxidil. Discontinue topical minoxidil after 3 months.
4Monitoring & Adjustments
  • Initial follow-up: CBC and liver function tests after 2 months.
  • Dose adjustment: If no improvement after 3 months at 2.5 mg, increase to 5 mg daily for another 6 months.
5Alternative Adjustments to Systemic Therapy
  • If finasteride is insufficient:
    • Substitute finasteride with dutasteride (Avodart) 0.5 mg on two non-consecutive days (e.g. Monday & Thursday).
    • Continue finasteride 1 mg on the other days (Tue, Wed, Fri, Sat, Sun).
    • Reassess after 6 months; consider daily dutasteride if no improvement.
Contraindications & Cautions (Oral Minoxidil):
  • Migraines: oral minoxidil may exacerbate.
  • Hypotension: patients with low blood pressure should not take oral minoxidil.
Conclusion:
  • Discontinuation: If no improvement after an extended period on the escalated dose, stop the medication.
  • Long-term success: If effective, continue indefinitely.

Female Pattern Alopecia

Stepwise protocol for women. Dosing differs from the male protocol and several systemic options are available beyond minoxidil.

1Topical Minoxidil 5% (younger women)
  • For children and women over 70: Use 2% solution once daily.
  • Application: Once daily to the scalp.
  • Duration: At least 6 months.
  • Evaluation: If no improvement after 6 months, add systemic therapy.
  • Contraindication: Do not prescribe if the patient has low blood pressure.
2Systemic — Oral Minoxidil
  • Initial dose: 0.75 mg per day.
  • Dose adjustment: Increase by 0.5 mg every 3 months if not effective, up to a maximum of 2.5 mg per day.
  • Procurement:
    • Prescription filled at regular pharmacies.
    • Order via "Tabechnik" using form "29ג" from "Shor Tabechnik".
  • Transition from topical to oral: Gradually reduce topical minoxidil from once daily to 2–3 times weekly over 2 months while initiating oral minoxidil. Discontinue topical minoxidil after 3 months.
3Monitoring
  • Initial follow-up: CBC and liver function tests after 2 months.

Alternative Systemic Options

Aldactone (Spironolactone)
  • Initial dose: 25 mg per day.
  • Dose adjustment: Increase by 25 mg per month until a maximum of 100 mg per day.
  • Special considerations: Particularly effective for women with hirsutism. Ensure no personal or family history of breast cancer.
Dutasteride
  • For postmenopausal women: Start with 0.5 mg twice a week; may increase to daily.
  • Cancer screening: Annual breast cancer screening is recommended.
Contraindications & Cautions (Oral Minoxidil):
  • Migraines: oral minoxidil may exacerbate.
  • Hypotension: patients with low blood pressure should not take oral minoxidil.
